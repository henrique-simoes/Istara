# Field Notes — Data Export and Reporting

**Research Goal:** Reduce average report generation time and eliminate top 3 export errors
**Product:** Fleet tracking and route optimization dashboard (ShipRoute Global)
**Researcher:** Field Research Team
**Period:** 2026-03-02 to 2026-03-15
**Sessions:** 4

## Session 1

**Date:** 2026-03-08
**Participant:** Carlos Mendoza (P06)
**Role:** Operations Manager, 500-2000 employees
**Setting:** Remote (Google Meet)
**Duration:** 60 minutes

### Observations

- Instinctively tried to drag-and-drop items in the list view.
- Navigated to the wrong section first, then self-corrected after 40 seconds.
- Used browser back button instead of in-app navigation on multiple occasions.
- Participant noted: "The loading time is really frustrating, especially when I'm trying to pull up something in a meeting"

### Notable Quotes

> "It took me way too long to figure out how to invite my team" — Carlos Mendoza

> "I wish more tools had this level of thought put into the empty states, it's clear what you need to do" — Carlos Mendoza

> "The customer support has been phenomenal, they responded to my issue in under an hour" — Carlos Mendoza

> "The tool doesn't warn me before I navigate away from an unsaved form" — Carlos Mendoza

### Task-Specific Notes

When asked: *"Have you ever had to apologize for a report error caused by the export process?"*

- Carlos Mendoza pulled up the product to demonstrate the issue in real time.

### Researcher Notes

*This participant represents a key segment — their confusion around navigation likely extends to other users in similar roles.*

## Cross-Session Themes

1. **Trust in data** — Participants want to rely on product reports but express uncertainty about accuracy.
2. **Feature discovery** — Powerful features go unused because participants never encounter them organically.
3. **Onboarding cliff** — Initial setup is praised, but participants describe feeling 'abandoned' after the first week.
4. **Workaround culture** — Participants have developed informal processes outside the product to compensate for gaps.
