# Diary Study — Data Export and Reporting

**Participant:** Mei-Lin Wu (P18)
**Role:** Design Director, 2000+ employees
**Product:** Patient portal and telehealth platform (HealthBridge)
**Study Topic:** Data Export and Reporting
**Research Goal:** Reduce average report generation time and eliminate top 3 export errors
**Date Range:** 2026-02-27 to 2026-03-12 (14 days)
**Methodology:** Longitudinal self-reported diary study with daily prompts

## Study Protocol

Participant was asked to log their interactions with the product at least once daily,
noting activities performed, emotional reactions, friction points encountered, and
overall mood. Photo/screenshot attachments were encouraged. Weekly reflections were
prompted every 7 days.

## End-of-Study Summary

### Mood Trend

- **Average mood:** 2.9 / 5
- **First half average:** 3.0 / 5
- **Second half average:** 2.8 / 5
- **Days logged:** 12 of 14
- **Overall trend:** Downward

### Emerging Themes

1. **Social learning dependency** — Participant relied heavily on coworkers for feature discovery, suggesting organic discoverability is insufficient.
2. **Trust erosion through data loss** — Instances of lost or unsaved work had a disproportionate impact on product trust that persisted for days afterward.
3. **Emotional habituation** — Strong initial reactions (positive and negative) faded over time, replaced by pragmatic acceptance.

### Key Participant Quotes

> "It feels like it was designed by engineers for engineers, not for someone like me" — Mei-Lin Wu, mid-study check-in

> "I can never remember where the billing page is buried" — Mei-Lin Wu, mid-study check-in

> "It took me way too long to figure out how to invite my team" — Mei-Lin Wu, Week 2 reflection

> "When I share a link with my team, they always have to log in again even if they already have accounts" — Mei-Lin Wu, Day 9

> "I genuinely look forward to using the analytics view, it's the best-designed part of the product" — Mei-Lin Wu, Week 2 reflection

### Recommendations

1. Redesign the mobile experience as a first-class companion app rather than a miniaturized desktop port.
2. Prioritize undo/redo functionality for destructive actions — this was a consistent source of anxiety throughout the study.
3. Implement intelligent notification grouping and quiet hours to reduce alert fatigue without losing important signals.
