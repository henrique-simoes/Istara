# Diary Study — Pricing Page Conversion

**Participant:** Marcus Johnson (P02)
**Role:** Engineering Director, 500-2000 employees
**Product:** Fleet tracking and route optimization dashboard (ShipRoute Global)
**Study Topic:** Pricing Page Conversion
**Research Goal:** Increase free-to-paid conversion rate from 3.1% to 5%
**Date Range:** 2026-01-29 to 2026-02-11 (14 days)
**Methodology:** Longitudinal self-reported diary study with daily prompts

## Study Protocol

Participant was asked to log their interactions with the product at least once daily,
noting activities performed, emotional reactions, friction points encountered, and
overall mood. Photo/screenshot attachments were encouraged. Weekly reflections were
prompted every 7 days.

## End-of-Study Summary

### Mood Trend

- **Average mood:** 3.3 / 5
- **First half average:** 2.5 / 5
- **Second half average:** 4.0 / 5
- **Days logged:** 12 of 14
- **Overall trend:** Upward

### Emerging Themes

1. **Workaround accumulation** — Over the study period, the participant developed 4-5 personal workarounds for product gaps, suggesting unmet feature needs.
2. **Context-switching tax** — Time lost transitioning between the product and complementary tools (Slack, email, docs) was a recurring theme.
3. **Notification fatigue arc** — Participant started engaged with notifications, then progressively muted channels until only critical alerts remained.
4. **Emotional habituation** — Strong initial reactions (positive and negative) faded over time, replaced by pragmatic acceptance.

### Key Participant Quotes

> "I can create a complete project plan in about twenty minutes that would have taken me half a day before" — Marcus Johnson, mid-study check-in

> "The maximum tag length is too short to be descriptive, so all our tags are cryptic abbreviations" — Marcus Johnson, Week 2 reflection

> "The weekly digest email is the one notification I actually look forward to getting" — Marcus Johnson, mid-study check-in

> "I accidentally deleted a project and there was no way to undo it" — Marcus Johnson, Week 2 reflection

### Recommendations

1. Add auto-save with visible version history to eliminate the anxiety around data loss.
2. Improve search relevance and surface recent items more prominently to support the 'find, don't navigate' behavior observed.
3. Standardize interaction patterns across product sections to reduce the cognitive load of context-switching within the app.
4. Implement intelligent notification grouping and quiet hours to reduce alert fatigue without losing important signals.
5. Redesign the mobile experience as a first-class companion app rather than a miniaturized desktop port.
