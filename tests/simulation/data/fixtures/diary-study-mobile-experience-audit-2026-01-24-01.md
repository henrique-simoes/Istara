# Diary Study — Mobile Experience Audit

**Participant:** Marcus Johnson (P02)
**Role:** Engineering Director, 500-2000 employees
**Product:** Collaborative learning management system (EduVerse)
**Study Topic:** Mobile Experience Audit
**Research Goal:** Understand why mobile session duration is 60% shorter than desktop
**Date Range:** 2026-01-24 to 2026-02-06 (14 days)
**Methodology:** Longitudinal self-reported diary study with daily prompts

## Study Protocol

Participant was asked to log their interactions with the product at least once daily,
noting activities performed, emotional reactions, friction points encountered, and
overall mood. Photo/screenshot attachments were encouraged. Weekly reflections were
prompted every 7 days.

## End-of-Study Summary

### Mood Trend

- **Average mood:** 2.6 / 5
- **First half average:** 2.0 / 5
- **Second half average:** 3.3 / 5
- **Days logged:** 14 of 14
- **Overall trend:** Upward

### Emerging Themes

1. **Workaround accumulation** — Over the study period, the participant developed 4-5 personal workarounds for product gaps, suggesting unmet feature needs.
2. **Notification fatigue arc** — Participant started engaged with notifications, then progressively muted channels until only critical alerts remained.
3. **Social learning dependency** — Participant relied heavily on coworkers for feature discovery, suggesting organic discoverability is insufficient.

### Key Participant Quotes

> "I keep a sticky note on my monitor with the steps to do the export because it's not intuitive at all" — Marcus Johnson, Week 2 reflection

> "The search bar basically never returns what I'm actually looking for" — Marcus Johnson, Week 2 reflection

> "I can't customize the columns in the table view, so I'm always scrolling sideways to find what I need" — Marcus Johnson, Day 3

### Recommendations

1. Prioritize undo/redo functionality for destructive actions — this was a consistent source of anxiety throughout the study.
2. Consider a 'quick actions' or command palette feature to reduce the navigation overhead participants experienced daily.
3. Create an in-product discovery mechanism (e.g., tips, contextual suggestions) to reduce dependency on social learning.
