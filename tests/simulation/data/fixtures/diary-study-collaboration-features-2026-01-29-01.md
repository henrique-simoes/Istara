# Diary Study — Collaboration Features

**Participant:** Sarah Chen (P01)
**Role:** Product Manager, 50-200 employees
**Product:** Expense management and invoicing tool (FinLedger)
**Study Topic:** Collaboration Features
**Research Goal:** Increase team workspace adoption from 23% to 50% of active accounts
**Date Range:** 2026-01-29 to 2026-02-11 (14 days)
**Methodology:** Longitudinal self-reported diary study with daily prompts

## Study Protocol

Participant was asked to log their interactions with the product at least once daily,
noting activities performed, emotional reactions, friction points encountered, and
overall mood. Photo/screenshot attachments were encouraged. Weekly reflections were
prompted every 7 days.

## End-of-Study Summary

### Mood Trend

- **Average mood:** 3.3 / 5
- **First half average:** 2.6 / 5
- **Second half average:** 4.0 / 5
- **Days logged:** 10 of 14
- **Overall trend:** Upward

### Emerging Themes

1. **Emotional habituation** — Strong initial reactions (positive and negative) faded over time, replaced by pragmatic acceptance.
2. **Mobile as second-class experience** — Mobile usage was limited to quick checks and never evolved into substantive work sessions, despite daily availability.
3. **Learning curve trajectory** — Initial confusion gave way to competence, but several interaction patterns remained unintuitive throughout the entire study period.
4. **Social learning dependency** — Participant relied heavily on coworkers for feature discovery, suggesting organic discoverability is insufficient.
5. **Feature depth vs. breadth** — Participant used a narrow set of features deeply rather than exploring the full product surface.

### Key Participant Quotes

> "It sends me way too many email notifications and I can't figure out how to turn them off individually" — Sarah Chen, mid-study check-in

> "I couldn't find where to change my settings, I ended up Googling it" — Sarah Chen, Week 2 reflection

> "I don't think the pricing page made it clear what I was actually getting" — Sarah Chen, Day 4

> "I recommended it to three other companies and two of them switched within a month" — Sarah Chen, Week 2 reflection

### Recommendations

1. Standardize interaction patterns across product sections to reduce the cognitive load of context-switching within the app.
2. Create an in-product discovery mechanism (e.g., tips, contextual suggestions) to reduce dependency on social learning.
3. Invest in performance optimization — even small delays compounded into significant frustration over a multi-week usage period.
4. Add auto-save with visible version history to eliminate the anxiety around data loss.
