# Field Notes — Pricing Page Conversion

**Research Goal:** Increase free-to-paid conversion rate from 3.1% to 5%
**Product:** Expense management and invoicing tool (FinLedger)
**Researcher:** Field Research Team
**Period:** 2026-02-21 to 2026-03-17
**Sessions:** 6

## Session 1

**Date:** 2026-03-09
**Participant:** Oluwaseun Adeyemi (P09)
**Role:** IT Administrator, 2000+ employees
**Setting:** Remote (phone call)
**Duration:** 38 minutes

**Environment:** Background noise from an open-plan office was noticeable — participant occasionally distracted.

### Observations

- Opened the settings panel and immediately closed it, saying it was overwhelming.
- Used Cmd+F (browser find) instead of the in-app search functionality.
- Paused for 15+ seconds at the form's second step, visibly confused by the field labels.
- Participant noted: "The error messages are completely useless, they just say something went wrong with no detail"

### Notable Quotes

> "I actually really liked how the dashboard shows everything at a glance" — Oluwaseun Adeyemi

> "I like that I can switch between Kanban and list view depending on what I'm working on that day" — Oluwaseun Adeyemi

> "The collaboration features are actually the main reason we chose this over the competitor" — Oluwaseun Adeyemi

> "The timeline view changed how our whole team plans sprints, it's been a game changer" — Oluwaseun Adeyemi

> "I can't drag tasks between different project boards, so I end up duplicating them manually" — Oluwaseun Adeyemi

### Task-Specific Notes

When asked: *"How do you justify the cost of this tool to your manager or finance team?"*

- Oluwaseun Adeyemi deferred to their team lead's experience on this topic.

### Researcher Notes

*This session reinforced the pattern from previous interviews — mobile is treated as view-only.*

## Cross-Session Themes

1. **Navigation confusion** — Multiple participants struggled to locate features they knew existed, resorting to browser search or help docs.
2. **Mobile avoidance** — Most participants described using mobile only for quick checks, not for substantive work.
3. **Trust in data** — Participants want to rely on product reports but express uncertainty about accuracy.
4. **Feature discovery** — Powerful features go unused because participants never encounter them organically.
