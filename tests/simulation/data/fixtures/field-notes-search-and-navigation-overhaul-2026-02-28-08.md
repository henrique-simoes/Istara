# Field Notes — Search and Navigation Overhaul

**Research Goal:** Reduce support tickets related to 'can't find feature' by 35%
**Product:** Project management platform (TechStart Inc)
**Researcher:** Field Research Team
**Period:** 2026-02-28 to 2026-03-16
**Sessions:** 4

## Session 1

**Date:** 2026-03-07
**Participant:** Aisha Rahman (P05)
**Role:** Junior Developer, 50-200 employees
**Setting:** Remote (Google Meet)
**Duration:** 31 minutes

**Environment:** Participant had the product open alongside three other browser tabs for context.

### Observations

- Opened the settings panel and immediately closed it, saying it was overwhelming.
- Used Cmd+F (browser find) instead of the in-app search functionality.
- Attempted to use keyboard shortcuts that don't exist in the product.
- Participant noted: "It sends me way too many email notifications and I can't figure out how to turn them off individually"

### Notable Quotes

> "The collaboration features are actually the main reason we chose this over the competitor" — Aisha Rahman

> "I ended up asking a coworker to walk me through it because the help docs didn't make sense" — Aisha Rahman

> "The onboarding emails were genuinely helpful, they came at the right pace" — Aisha Rahman

### Researcher Notes

*The participant's workflow involves steps outside our product that could be integrated.*

## Cross-Session Themes

1. **Collaboration gaps** — Teams resort to external tools (Slack, email) for tasks the product should support.
2. **Notification fatigue** — Across sessions, participants described turning off most notifications due to volume.
3. **Mobile avoidance** — Most participants described using mobile only for quick checks, not for substantive work.
4. **Feature discovery** — Powerful features go unused because participants never encounter them organically.
5. **Onboarding cliff** — Initial setup is praised, but participants describe feeling 'abandoned' after the first week.
