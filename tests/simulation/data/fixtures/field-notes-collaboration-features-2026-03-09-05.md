# Field Notes — Collaboration Features

**Research Goal:** Increase team workspace adoption from 23% to 50% of active accounts
**Product:** Collaborative learning management system (EduVerse)
**Researcher:** Field Research Team
**Period:** 2026-03-09 to 2026-03-06
**Sessions:** 4

## Session 1

**Date:** 2026-03-10
**Participant:** Priya Patel (P03)
**Role:** UX Designer, 10-50 employees
**Setting:** In-person (coffee shop)
**Duration:** 59 minutes

### Observations

- Ignored the tooltip entirely and guessed at the button's function correctly.
- Opened the help menu within the first 30 seconds of the session.
- Participant immediately looked for a search bar upon landing on the page.
- Participant scrolled past the primary CTA three times before noticing it.
- Participant noted: "The dashboard widgets rearrange themselves every time I log in, which is disorienting"

### Notable Quotes

> "I was pleasantly surprised by how fast the search results came back" — Priya Patel

## Cross-Session Themes

1. **Feature discovery** — Powerful features go unused because participants never encounter them organically.
2. **Navigation confusion** — Multiple participants struggled to locate features they knew existed, resorting to browser search or help docs.
3. **Mobile avoidance** — Most participants described using mobile only for quick checks, not for substantive work.
4. **Notification fatigue** — Across sessions, participants described turning off most notifications due to volume.
5. **Collaboration gaps** — Teams resort to external tools (Slack, email) for tasks the product should support.
