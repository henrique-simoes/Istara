# Field Notes — Dashboard Usability

**Research Goal:** Improve time-to-insight from average 4.2 minutes to under 2 minutes
**Product:** Collaborative learning management system (EduVerse)
**Researcher:** Field Research Team
**Period:** 2026-02-20 to 2026-03-12
**Sessions:** 6

## Session 1

**Date:** 2026-03-10
**Participant:** Hannah Eriksson (P22)
**Role:** Content Strategist, 10-50 employees
**Setting:** Remote (phone call)
**Duration:** 38 minutes

### Observations

- Completed the task faster than expected but expressed low confidence in the result.
- Opened the help menu within the first 30 seconds of the session.
- Spent over 2 minutes reading the empty state message before taking action.
- Participant noted: "The help docs are always out of date and show screenshots from an older version"

### Notable Quotes

> "It does what I need about eighty percent of the time, the other twenty percent I work around" — Hannah Eriksson

> "The mobile app is fine for checking things, but I'd never try to do real work on it" — Hannah Eriksson

> "Switching between projects is painfully slow, there's no quick switcher" — Hannah Eriksson

### Researcher Notes

*There's a clear gap between what we consider 'discoverable' and what users actually find without guidance.*

## Session 2

**Date:** 2026-03-05
**Participant:** James O'Brien (P04)
**Role:** Sales Lead, 200-500 employees
**Setting:** Remote (Zoom)
**Duration:** 60 minutes

### Observations

- Instinctively tried to drag-and-drop items in the list view.
- Asked aloud 'Where did that go?' after a modal closed unexpectedly.
- Opened the settings panel and immediately closed it, saying it was overwhelming.
- Opened the help menu within the first 30 seconds of the session.
- Asked whether the page had finished loading — no clear loading indicator was present.
- Participant immediately looked for a search bar upon landing on the page.
- Participant noted: "Every time I export a report, the formatting is completely off and I have to fix it in Excel"

### Notable Quotes

> "Honestly, I almost gave up during the sign-up process" — James O'Brien

> "I had to watch a YouTube tutorial to understand the reporting feature, and that shouldn't be necessary" — James O'Brien

> "I remember thinking, why is this taking so many clicks?" — James O'Brien

> "I use it every single day, so even small annoyances add up over time" — James O'Brien

> "When I share a link with my team, they always have to log in again even if they already have accounts" — James O'Brien

### Task-Specific Notes

When asked: *"If you could rearrange the dashboard, what would you change?"*

- James O'Brien immediately launched into a detailed answer, suggesting this is a frequent concern.

### Researcher Notes

*The emotional reaction to the error state was stronger than anticipated. Worth revisiting the error copy.*

## Session 4

**Date:** 2026-03-06
**Participant:** Carlos Mendoza (P06)
**Role:** Operations Manager, 500-2000 employees
**Setting:** In-person (office)
**Duration:** 43 minutes

### Observations

- Verbalized frustration after the third failed attempt at the same task.
- Tried to resize a panel that was fixed-width, suggesting expectations from other tools.
- Expressed surprise that undoing an action was not possible.
- Tried to right-click for additional options, found none available.
- Bookmarked a specific page because 'I'll never find it again through the nav.'
- Participant noted: "I keep getting logged out randomly in the middle of my work"

### Notable Quotes

> "I think the biggest issue is just consistency, some parts feel modern and others feel like they haven't been updated in years" — Carlos Mendoza

> "Once I got the hang of it, the workflow felt pretty natural" — Carlos Mendoza

> "The drag-and-drop interface is really satisfying when it works, but sometimes items just snap to the wrong place" — Carlos Mendoza

> "The search bar basically never returns what I'm actually looking for" — Carlos Mendoza

### Task-Specific Notes

When asked: *"Walk me through how you typically start your day with the dashboard."*

- Carlos Mendoza paused to think for several seconds before responding.

### Researcher Notes

*The participant's workflow involves steps outside our product that could be integrated.*

## Session 6

**Date:** 2026-02-23
**Participant:** Priya Patel (P03)
**Role:** UX Designer, 10-50 employees
**Setting:** In-person (conference room)
**Duration:** 55 minutes

**Environment:** Strong overhead lighting caused screen glare for the participant.

### Observations

- Opened the settings panel and immediately closed it, saying it was overwhelming.
- Commented positively on the visual design but struggled with the interaction model.
- Used browser back button instead of in-app navigation on multiple occasions.

### Notable Quotes

> "I actually really liked how the dashboard shows everything at a glance" — Priya Patel

> "The fact that I can't undo a bulk action makes me nervous every time I use it" — Priya Patel

### Researcher Notes

*This session reinforced the pattern from previous interviews — mobile is treated as view-only.*

## Cross-Session Themes

1. **Terminology mismatch** — The vocabulary used by participants differs from product labels, causing friction.
2. **Workaround culture** — Participants have developed informal processes outside the product to compensate for gaps.
3. **Mobile avoidance** — Most participants described using mobile only for quick checks, not for substantive work.
