# Field Notes — Data Export and Reporting

**Research Goal:** Reduce average report generation time and eliminate top 3 export errors
**Product:** Patient portal and telehealth platform (HealthBridge)
**Researcher:** Field Research Team
**Period:** 2026-03-07 to 2026-03-11
**Sessions:** 3

## Session 1

**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Participant:** James O'Brien (P04)
**Role:** Sales Lead, 200-500 employees
**Setting:** Remote (Zoom)
**Duration:** 44 minutes

### Observations

- Completed the task faster than expected but expressed low confidence in the result.
- Opened the help menu within the first 30 seconds of the session.
- Spent over 2 minutes reading the empty state message before taking action.
- Switched between two tabs repeatedly to compare information.
- Used browser back button instead of in-app navigation on multiple occasions.
- Participant noted: "The error messages are completely useless, they just say something went wrong with no detail"

### Notable Quotes

> "I remember thinking, why is this taking so many clicks?" — James O'Brien

> "Once I got the hang of it, the workflow felt pretty natural" — James O'Brien

> "The fact that I can't undo a bulk action makes me nervous every time I use it" — James O'Brien

> "The onboarding tutorial was so long I just skipped it, and then I was completely lost" — James O'Brien

### Task-Specific Notes

When asked: *"Have you ever run into errors or unexpected results during export?"*

- James O'Brien immediately launched into a detailed answer, suggesting this is a frequent concern.

### Researcher Notes

*The emotional reaction to the error state was stronger than anticipated. Worth revisiting the error copy.*

## Session 2

**Date:** 2026-03-12
**Participant:** Carlos Mendoza (P06)
**Role:** Operations Manager, 500-2000 employees
**Setting:** Remote (phone call)
**Duration:** 54 minutes

**Environment:** Strong overhead lighting caused screen glare for the participant.

### Observations

- Bookmarked a specific page because 'I'll never find it again through the nav.'
- Participant scrolled past the primary CTA three times before noticing it.
- Commented positively on the visual design but struggled with the interaction model.
- Attempted to use keyboard shortcuts that don't exist in the product.
- Leaned forward and squinted at the screen, suggesting readability issues.
- Participant noted: "I can never remember where the billing page is buried"

### Notable Quotes

> "I had to watch a YouTube tutorial to understand the reporting feature, and that shouldn't be necessary" — Carlos Mendoza

> "Compared to what we used before, this is a huge improvement, but the bar was pretty low" — Carlos Mendoza

> "There's no keyboard shortcut for the one action I do fifty times a day" — Carlos Mendoza

### Task-Specific Notes

When asked: *"What information do you wish was easier to pull out of the system?"*

- Carlos Mendoza referred to a specific incident from last week as an example.

### Researcher Notes

*Participant's mental model of the product hierarchy does not match our information architecture.*

## Session 3

**Date:** 2026-02-27
**Participant:** Raj Krishnamurthy (P21)
**Role:** Engineering Manager, 200-500 employees
**Setting:** Remote (Google Meet)
**Duration:** 49 minutes

**Environment:** Stable internet connection throughout the session — no technical disruptions.

### Observations

- Participant scrolled past the primary CTA three times before noticing it.
- Tried to right-click for additional options, found none available.
- Instinctively tried to drag-and-drop items in the list view.

### Notable Quotes

> "I was confused by the terminology, what you call a workspace I would call a project" — Raj Krishnamurthy

### Researcher Notes

*Participant's workaround reveals an unmet need that could become a feature request.*

## Cross-Session Themes

1. **Notification fatigue** — Across sessions, participants described turning off most notifications due to volume.
2. **Workaround culture** — Participants have developed informal processes outside the product to compensate for gaps.
3. **Trust in data** — Participants want to rely on product reports but express uncertainty about accuracy.
