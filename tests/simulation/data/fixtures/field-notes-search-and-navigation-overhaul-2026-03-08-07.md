# Field Notes — Search and Navigation Overhaul

**Research Goal:** Reduce support tickets related to 'can't find feature' by 35%
**Product:** Patient portal and telehealth platform (HealthBridge)
**Researcher:** Field Research Team
**Period:** 2026-03-08 to 2026-03-16
**Sessions:** 3

## Session 1

**Date:** 2026-03-14
**Participant:** Yuki Sato (P28)
**Role:** Mobile Developer, 10-50 employees
**Setting:** Remote (Zoom)
**Duration:** 60 minutes

### Observations

- Tried to right-click for additional options, found none available.
- Opened the help menu within the first 30 seconds of the session.
- Expressed surprise that undoing an action was not possible.
- Used Cmd+F (browser find) instead of the in-app search functionality.
- Switched between two tabs repeatedly to compare information.
- Participant noted: "The dependency arrows in the Gantt chart overlap so much they're completely unreadable"

### Notable Quotes

> "It took maybe fifteen minutes for the whole team to understand the basic concepts, which is impressive" — Yuki Sato

> "I tried to set it up for my personal projects and it felt like overkill, it's really built for teams" — Yuki Sato

> "My team actually enjoys doing status updates now because the interface makes it quick and painless" — Yuki Sato

> "I can tell the team that built this actually uses their own product, the little details show it" — Yuki Sato

### Researcher Notes

*Participant's workaround reveals an unmet need that could become a feature request.*

## Session 3

**Date:** 2026-03-07
**Participant:** Catherine Dubois (P29)
**Role:** Head of Training, 2000+ employees
**Setting:** Remote (Zoom)
**Duration:** 50 minutes

**Environment:** Strong overhead lighting caused screen glare for the participant.

### Observations

- Opened the settings panel and immediately closed it, saying it was overwhelming.
- Ignored the tooltip entirely and guessed at the button's function correctly.
- Navigated to the wrong section first, then self-corrected after 40 seconds.
- Clicked the same button twice rapidly, triggering a duplicate submission.
- Participant scrolled past the primary CTA three times before noticing it.
- Participant noted: "The dependency arrows in the Gantt chart overlap so much they're completely unreadable"

### Notable Quotes

> "The integration with our CI/CD pipeline means developers never have to leave the tool to update task status" — Catherine Dubois

> "I think the mobile notifications could be smarter, right now I get alerts for things that don't need my attention" — Catherine Dubois

> "I tried to merge two duplicate tasks and lost all the comments from one of them" — Catherine Dubois

### Researcher Notes

*This session reinforced the pattern from previous interviews — mobile is treated as view-only.*

## Cross-Session Themes

1. **Notification fatigue** — Across sessions, participants described turning off most notifications due to volume.
2. **Performance sensitivity** — Even small delays (2-3 seconds) are perceived as significant blockers.
3. **Terminology mismatch** — The vocabulary used by participants differs from product labels, causing friction.
4. **Mobile avoidance** — Most participants described using mobile only for quick checks, not for substantive work.
