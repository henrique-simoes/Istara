**Participant:** Lisa Johansson (P26)
**Role:** Scrum Master, 500-2000 employees
**Product:** Collaborative learning management system (EduVerse)
**Study Topic:** Mobile Experience Audit
**Research Goal:** Understand why mobile session duration is 60% shorter than desktop
**Date Range:** 2026-03-15 to 2026-03-28 (14 days)
**Methodology:** Longitudinal self-reported diary study with daily prompts

## Study Protocol

Participant was asked to log their interactions with the product at least once daily,
noting activities performed, emotional reactions, friction points encountered, and
overall mood. Photo/screenshot attachments were encouraged. Weekly reflections were
prompted every 7 days.

## End-of-Study Summary

### Mood Trend

- **Average mood:** 2.9 / 5
- **First half average:** 2.3 / 5
- **Second half average:** 3.7 / 5
- **Days logged:** 13 of 14
- **Overall trend:** Upward

### Emerging Themes

1. **Workaround accumulation** — Over the study period, the participant developed 4-5 personal workarounds for product gaps, suggesting unmet feature needs.
2. **Trust erosion through data loss** — Instances of lost or unsaved work had a disproportionate impact on product trust that persisted for days afterward.
3. **Context-switching tax** — Time lost transitioning between the product and complementary tools (Slack, email, docs) was a recurring theme.

### Key Participant Quotes

> "The collaboration features are actually the main reason we chose this over the competitor" — Lisa Johansson, mid-study check-in

> "I genuinely look forward to using the analytics view, it's the best-designed part of the product" — Lisa Johansson, Day 7

> "I ended up asking a coworker to walk me through it because the help docs didn't make sense" — Lisa Johansson, mid-study check-in

> "It feels like it was designed by engineers for engineers, not for someone like me" — Lisa Johansson, mid-study check-in

> "I'm not sure who decided the default view should show everything, it's completely overwhelming" — Lisa Johansson, Week 2 reflection

### Recommendations

1. Create an in-product discovery mechanism (e.g., tips, contextual suggestions) to reduce dependency on social learning.
2. Invest in progressive onboarding that introduces advanced features after core workflows are established, not during initial setup.
3. Improve search relevance and surface recent items more prominently to support the 'find, don't navigate' behavior observed.
4. Redesign the mobile experience as a first-class companion app rather than a miniaturized desktop port.
5. Prioritize undo/redo functionality for destructive actions — this was a consistent source of anxiety throughout the study.
