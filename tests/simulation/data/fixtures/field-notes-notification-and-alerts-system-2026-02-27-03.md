# Field Notes — Notification and Alerts System

**Research Goal:** Decrease notification opt-out rate from 68% to under 40%
**Product:** Collaborative learning management system (EduVerse)
**Researcher:** Field Research Team
**Period:** 2026-02-27 to 2026-03-06
**Sessions:** 5

## Session 1

**Date:** 2026-03-07
**Participant:** Catherine Dubois (P29)
**Role:** Head of Training, 2000+ employees
**Setting:** In-person (office)
**Duration:** 32 minutes

**Environment:** Stable internet connection throughout the session — no technical disruptions.

### Observations

- Completed the task faster than expected but expressed low confidence in the result.
- Opened the settings panel and immediately closed it, saying it was overwhelming.
- Attempted to use keyboard shortcuts that don't exist in the product.
- Smiled and nodded when the success confirmation appeared — clear positive reaction.
- Tried to right-click for additional options, found none available.
- Participant noted: "The onboarding tutorial was so long I just skipped it, and then I was completely lost"

### Notable Quotes

> "When I saw the new design, my first reaction was, where did everything go?" — Catherine Dubois

> "I had to watch a YouTube tutorial to understand the reporting feature, and that shouldn't be necessary" — Catherine Dubois

> "Setting up the integration was a breeze, probably the smoothest part of the whole experience" — Catherine Dubois

### Researcher Notes

*This session reinforced the pattern from previous interviews — mobile is treated as view-only.*

## Cross-Session Themes

1. **Terminology mismatch** — The vocabulary used by participants differs from product labels, causing friction.
2. **Workaround culture** — Participants have developed informal processes outside the product to compensate for gaps.
3. **Collaboration gaps** — Teams resort to external tools (Slack, email) for tasks the product should support.
