# Field Notes — Notification and Alerts System

**Research Goal:** Decrease notification opt-out rate from 68% to under 40%
**Product:** Fleet tracking and route optimization dashboard (ShipRoute Global)
**Researcher:** Field Research Team
**Period:** 2026-02-28 to 2026-03-11
**Sessions:** 5

## Session 1

**Date:** 2026-03-11
**Participant:** Catherine Dubois (P29)
**Role:** Head of Training, 2000+ employees
**Setting:** Remote (phone call)
**Duration:** 59 minutes

**Environment:** Participant frequently multitasked, checking Slack during brief pauses.

### Observations

- Participant scrolled past the primary CTA three times before noticing it.
- Asked whether the page had finished loading — no clear loading indicator was present.
- Attempted to use keyboard shortcuts that don't exist in the product.
- Clicked the same button twice rapidly, triggering a duplicate submission.
- Spent over 2 minutes reading the empty state message before taking action.
- Bookmarked a specific page because 'I'll never find it again through the nav.'
- Participant noted: "The onboarding tutorial was so long I just skipped it, and then I was completely lost"

### Notable Quotes

> "I don't think the pricing page made it clear what I was actually getting" — Catherine Dubois

> "I remember thinking, why is this taking so many clicks?" — Catherine Dubois

> "I would absolutely recommend it to my team, with a few caveats" — Catherine Dubois

> "I accidentally sent an incomplete report to a client because the save and send buttons are right next to each other" — Catherine Dubois

### Researcher Notes

*The participant's workflow involves steps outside our product that could be integrated.*

## Cross-Session Themes

1. **Terminology mismatch** — The vocabulary used by participants differs from product labels, causing friction.
2. **Trust in data** — Participants want to rely on product reports but express uncertainty about accuracy.
3. **Performance sensitivity** — Even small delays (2-3 seconds) are perceived as significant blockers.
