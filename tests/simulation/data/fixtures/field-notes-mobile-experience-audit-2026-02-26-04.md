# Field Notes — Mobile Experience Audit

**Research Goal:** Understand why mobile session duration is 60% shorter than desktop
**Product:** Patient portal and telehealth platform (HealthBridge)
**Researcher:** Field Research Team
**Period:** 2026-02-26 to 2026-03-12
**Sessions:** 5

## Session 1

**Date:** 2026-03-06
**Participant:** Yuki Sato (P28)
**Role:** Mobile Developer, 10-50 employees
**Setting:** Remote (Zoom)
**Duration:** 31 minutes

**Environment:** Participant frequently multitasked, checking Slack during brief pauses.

### Observations

- Leaned forward and squinted at the screen, suggesting readability issues.
- Switched between two tabs repeatedly to compare information.
- Opened the help menu within the first 30 seconds of the session.
- Participant noted: "The calendar view doesn't show tasks that span multiple days correctly, they just appear on the start date"

### Notable Quotes

> "I tried to set it up for my personal projects and it felt like overkill, it's really built for teams" — Yuki Sato

> "I actually really liked how the dashboard shows everything at a glance" — Yuki Sato

> "The dark mode has contrast issues that make some status badges completely invisible" — Yuki Sato

### Researcher Notes

*The participant's workflow involves steps outside our product that could be integrated.*

## Cross-Session Themes

1. **Onboarding cliff** — Initial setup is praised, but participants describe feeling 'abandoned' after the first week.
2. **Trust in data** — Participants want to rely on product reports but express uncertainty about accuracy.
3. **Notification fatigue** — Across sessions, participants described turning off most notifications due to volume.
