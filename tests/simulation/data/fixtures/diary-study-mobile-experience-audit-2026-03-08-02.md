# Diary Study — Mobile Experience Audit

**Participant:** Zainab Osei (P24)
**Role:** UX Researcher, 50-200 employees
**Product:** Patient portal and telehealth platform (HealthBridge)
**Study Topic:** Mobile Experience Audit
**Research Goal:** Understand why mobile session duration is 60% shorter than desktop
**Date Range:** 2026-03-08 to 2026-03-21 (14 days)
**Methodology:** Longitudinal self-reported diary study with daily prompts

## Study Protocol

Participant was asked to log their interactions with the product at least once daily,
noting activities performed, emotional reactions, friction points encountered, and
overall mood. Photo/screenshot attachments were encouraged. Weekly reflections were
prompted every 7 days.

## End-of-Study Summary

### Mood Trend

- **Average mood:** 2.9 / 5
- **First half average:** 2.6 / 5
- **Second half average:** 3.3 / 5
- **Days logged:** 14 of 14
- **Overall trend:** Upward

### Emerging Themes

1. **Learning curve trajectory** — Initial confusion gave way to competence, but several interaction patterns remained unintuitive throughout the entire study period.
2. **Notification fatigue arc** — Participant started engaged with notifications, then progressively muted channels until only critical alerts remained.
3. **Social learning dependency** — Participant relied heavily on coworkers for feature discovery, suggesting organic discoverability is insufficient.

### Key Participant Quotes

> "I love the keyboard shortcuts, they save me so much time" — Zainab Osei, mid-study check-in

> "Compared to what we used before, this is a huge improvement, but the bar was pretty low" — Zainab Osei, mid-study check-in

> "Setting up the integration was a breeze, probably the smoothest part of the whole experience" — Zainab Osei, Day 13

> "Every time I export a report, the formatting is completely off and I have to fix it in Excel" — Zainab Osei, Day 6

> "It does what I need about eighty percent of the time, the other twenty percent I work around" — Zainab Osei, Week 2 reflection

### Recommendations

1. Invest in progressive onboarding that introduces advanced features after core workflows are established, not during initial setup.
2. Redesign the mobile experience as a first-class companion app rather than a miniaturized desktop port.
3. Implement intelligent notification grouping and quiet hours to reduce alert fatigue without losing important signals.
