# Field Notes — Pricing Page Conversion

**Research Goal:** Increase free-to-paid conversion rate from 3.1% to 5%
**Product:** Patient portal and telehealth platform (HealthBridge)
**Researcher:** Field Research Team
**Period:** 2026-02-27 to 2026-03-12
**Sessions:** 3

## Session 1

**Date:** 2026-03-06
**Participant:** Pavel Volkov (P25)
**Role:** DevOps Lead, 200-500 employees
**Setting:** Remote (phone call)
**Duration:** 53 minutes

**Environment:** Participant's monitor was smaller than expected (~13"), affecting layout perception.

### Observations

- Expressed surprise that undoing an action was not possible.
- Asked aloud 'Where did that go?' after a modal closed unexpectedly.
- Instinctively tried to drag-and-drop items in the list view.
- Spent over 2 minutes reading the empty state message before taking action.
- Participant noted: "I can't drag tasks between different project boards, so I end up duplicating them manually"

### Notable Quotes

> "I think the biggest issue is just consistency, some parts feel modern and others feel like they haven't been updated in years" — Pavel Volkov

> "The way archived projects are handled is clean, they're out of the way but easy to find when you need them" — Pavel Volkov

### Task-Specific Notes

When asked: *"What feature would make you upgrade to the next tier immediately?"*

- Pavel Volkov paused to think for several seconds before responding.

## Session 2

**Date:** 2026-03-08
**Participant:** Catherine Dubois (P29)
**Role:** Head of Training, 2000+ employees
**Setting:** Remote (Zoom)
**Duration:** 44 minutes

### Observations

- Participant scrolled past the primary CTA three times before noticing it.
- Completed the task faster than expected but expressed low confidence in the result.
- Spent over 2 minutes reading the empty state message before taking action.
- Bookmarked a specific page because 'I'll never find it again through the nav.'
- Participant immediately looked for a search bar upon landing on the page.
- Switched between two tabs repeatedly to compare information.
- Participant noted: "When I collapse a section, it re-expands every time I come back to the page"

### Notable Quotes

> "The timeline view changed how our whole team plans sprints, it's been a game changer" — Catherine Dubois

> "I think the mobile notifications could be smarter, right now I get alerts for things that don't need my attention" — Catherine Dubois

> "I can create a complete project plan in about twenty minutes that would have taken me half a day before" — Catherine Dubois

> "The date picker doesn't let me type a date manually, I have to click through months one by one" — Catherine Dubois

### Researcher Notes

*This participant represents a key segment — their confusion around navigation likely extends to other users in similar roles.*

## Cross-Session Themes

1. **Workaround culture** — Participants have developed informal processes outside the product to compensate for gaps.
2. **Notification fatigue** — Across sessions, participants described turning off most notifications due to volume.
3. **Performance sensitivity** — Even small delays (2-3 seconds) are perceived as significant blockers.
4. **Mobile avoidance** — Most participants described using mobile only for quick checks, not for substantive work.
