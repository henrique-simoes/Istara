# Field Notes — Notification and Alerts System

**Research Goal:** Decrease notification opt-out rate from 68% to under 40%
**Product:** Project management platform (TechStart Inc)
**Researcher:** Field Research Team
**Period:** 2026-02-22 to 2026-03-15
**Sessions:** 5

## Session 1

**Date:** 2026-03-04
**Participant:** Tomás García (P13)
**Role:** Project Manager, 200-500 employees
**Setting:** Remote (phone call)
**Duration:** 34 minutes

**Environment:** Stable internet connection throughout the session — no technical disruptions.

### Observations

- Attempted to use keyboard shortcuts that don't exist in the product.
- Participant scrolled past the primary CTA three times before noticing it.
- Used Cmd+F (browser find) instead of the in-app search functionality.
- Spent over 2 minutes reading the empty state message before taking action.
- Paused for 15+ seconds at the form's second step, visibly confused by the field labels.
- Participant noted: "The auto-save never seems to work when I need it most, I've lost entire paragraphs of notes"

### Notable Quotes

> "I recommended it to three other companies and two of them switched within a month" — Tomás García

> "Our team adopted it in about two days, which is really fast compared to other tools we've tried" — Tomás García

> "The collaboration features are actually the main reason we chose this over the competitor" — Tomás García

> "I actually really liked how the dashboard shows everything at a glance" — Tomás García

### Researcher Notes

*This session reinforced the pattern from previous interviews — mobile is treated as view-only.*

## Cross-Session Themes

1. **Navigation confusion** — Multiple participants struggled to locate features they knew existed, resorting to browser search or help docs.
2. **Collaboration gaps** — Teams resort to external tools (Slack, email) for tasks the product should support.
3. **Trust in data** — Participants want to rely on product reports but express uncertainty about accuracy.
