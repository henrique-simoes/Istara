# Field Notes — Onboarding Redesign

**Research Goal:** Reduce 45% drop-off at phone verification step
**Product:** Expense management and invoicing tool (FinLedger)
**Researcher:** Field Research Team
**Period:** 2026-03-01 to 2026-03-14
**Sessions:** 6

## Session 1

**Date:** 2026-03-08
**Participant:** Wei Zhang (P08)
**Role:** Data Analyst, 50-200 employees
**Setting:** In-person (coffee shop)
**Duration:** 48 minutes

**Environment:** Participant used a trackpad rather than a mouse, influencing interaction precision.

### Observations

- Verbalized frustration after the third failed attempt at the same task.
- Opened the help menu within the first 30 seconds of the session.
- Participant immediately looked for a search bar upon landing on the page.
- Participant noted: "The autocomplete in the search field suggests results from archived projects that aren't relevant anymore"

### Notable Quotes

> "I actually really liked how the dashboard shows everything at a glance" — Wei Zhang

> "I remember thinking, why is this taking so many clicks?" — Wei Zhang

> "The timeline view changed how our whole team plans sprints, it's been a game changer" — Wei Zhang

> "The activity feed gives me a clear picture of what happened while I was away without reading every message" — Wei Zhang

### Task-Specific Notes

When asked: *"At what point did you feel confident you could use the product?"*

- Wei Zhang immediately launched into a detailed answer, suggesting this is a frequent concern.

### Researcher Notes

*Consider recruiting more participants from enterprise-sized companies — their patterns may differ.*

## Cross-Session Themes

1. **Navigation confusion** — Multiple participants struggled to locate features they knew existed, resorting to browser search or help docs.
2. **Collaboration gaps** — Teams resort to external tools (Slack, email) for tasks the product should support.
3. **Mobile avoidance** — Most participants described using mobile only for quick checks, not for substantive work.
4. **Trust in data** — Participants want to rely on product reports but express uncertainty about accuracy.
