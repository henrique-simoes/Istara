# Diary Study — Mobile Experience Audit

**Participant:** David Okonkwo (P11)
**Role:** VP of Product, 500-2000 employees
**Product:** Expense management and invoicing tool (FinLedger)
**Study Topic:** Mobile Experience Audit
**Research Goal:** Understand why mobile session duration is 60% shorter than desktop
**Date Range:** 2026-02-27 to 2026-03-12 (14 days)
**Methodology:** Longitudinal self-reported diary study with daily prompts

## Study Protocol

Participant was asked to log their interactions with the product at least once daily,
noting activities performed, emotional reactions, friction points encountered, and
overall mood. Photo/screenshot attachments were encouraged. Weekly reflections were
prompted every 7 days.

## End-of-Study Summary

### Mood Trend

- **Average mood:** 3.1 / 5
- **First half average:** 2.9 / 5
- **Second half average:** 3.4 / 5
- **Days logged:** 14 of 14
- **Overall trend:** Upward

### Emerging Themes

1. **Learning curve trajectory** — Initial confusion gave way to competence, but several interaction patterns remained unintuitive throughout the entire study period.
2. **Mobile as second-class experience** — Mobile usage was limited to quick checks and never evolved into substantive work sessions, despite daily availability.
3. **Notification fatigue arc** — Participant started engaged with notifications, then progressively muted channels until only critical alerts remained.
4. **Workaround accumulation** — Over the study period, the participant developed 4-5 personal workarounds for product gaps, suggesting unmet feature needs.
5. **Trust erosion through data loss** — Instances of lost or unsaved work had a disproportionate impact on product trust that persisted for days afterward.

### Key Participant Quotes

> "The way it handles recurring tasks is finally how I always thought it should work in other tools" — David Okonkwo, mid-study check-in

> "I'm not sure who decided the default view should show everything, it's completely overwhelming" — David Okonkwo, Week 2 reflection

> "The guest access feature is perfect for when we need to loop in contractors or clients temporarily" — David Okonkwo, mid-study check-in

### Recommendations

1. Create an in-product discovery mechanism (e.g., tips, contextual suggestions) to reduce dependency on social learning.
2. Prioritize undo/redo functionality for destructive actions — this was a consistent source of anxiety throughout the study.
3. Improve search relevance and surface recent items more prominently to support the 'find, don't navigate' behavior observed.
