# Field Notes — Collaboration Features

**Research Goal:** Increase team workspace adoption from 23% to 50% of active accounts
**Product:** Fleet tracking and route optimization dashboard (ShipRoute Global)
**Researcher:** Field Research Team
**Period:** 2026-03-06 to 2026-03-11
**Sessions:** 4

## Session 1

**Date:** 2026-03-09
**Participant:** Oluwaseun Adeyemi (P09)
**Role:** IT Administrator, 2000+ employees
**Setting:** In-person (coffee shop)
**Duration:** 39 minutes

**Environment:** Participant joined 5 minutes late due to calendar confusion.

### Observations

- Instinctively tried to drag-and-drop items in the list view.
- Commented positively on the visual design but struggled with the interaction model.
- Ignored the tooltip entirely and guessed at the button's function correctly.
- Paused for 15+ seconds at the form's second step, visibly confused by the field labels.
- Leaned forward and squinted at the screen, suggesting readability issues.
- Participant noted: "The dashboard widgets rearrange themselves every time I log in, which is disorienting"

### Notable Quotes

> "I was confused by the terminology, what you call a workspace I would call a project" — Oluwaseun Adeyemi

> "I love the keyboard shortcuts, they save me so much time" — Oluwaseun Adeyemi

> "I keep getting logged out randomly in the middle of my work" — Oluwaseun Adeyemi

### Task-Specific Notes

When asked: *"What would make it easier to get your whole team on board?"*

- Oluwaseun Adeyemi laughed and said this was the number-one thing they'd change.

### Researcher Notes

*The think-aloud protocol worked well here. Participant was naturally verbose and articulate.*

## Cross-Session Themes

1. **Navigation confusion** — Multiple participants struggled to locate features they knew existed, resorting to browser search or help docs.
2. **Trust in data** — Participants want to rely on product reports but express uncertainty about accuracy.
3. **Collaboration gaps** — Teams resort to external tools (Slack, email) for tasks the product should support.
4. **Workaround culture** — Participants have developed informal processes outside the product to compensate for gaps.
5. **Feature discovery** — Powerful features go unused because participants never encounter them organically.
