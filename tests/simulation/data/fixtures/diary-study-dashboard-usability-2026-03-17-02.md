# Diary Study — Dashboard Usability

**Participant:** Naomi Tanaka (P14)
**Role:** Research Lead, 500-2000 employees
**Product:** Patient portal and telehealth platform (HealthBridge)
**Study Topic:** Dashboard Usability
**Research Goal:** Improve time-to-insight from average 4.2 minutes to under 2 minutes
**Date Range:** 2026-03-17 to 2026-03-30 (14 days)
**Methodology:** Longitudinal self-reported diary study with daily prompts

## Study Protocol

Participant was asked to log their interactions with the product at least once daily,
noting activities performed, emotional reactions, friction points encountered, and
overall mood. Photo/screenshot attachments were encouraged. Weekly reflections were
prompted every 7 days.

## End-of-Study Summary

### Mood Trend

- **Average mood:** 3.0 / 5
- **First half average:** 2.5 / 5
- **Second half average:** 3.5 / 5
- **Days logged:** 12 of 14
- **Overall trend:** Upward

### Emerging Themes

1. **Trust erosion through data loss** — Instances of lost or unsaved work had a disproportionate impact on product trust that persisted for days afterward.
2. **Context-switching tax** — Time lost transitioning between the product and complementary tools (Slack, email, docs) was a recurring theme.
3. **Workaround accumulation** — Over the study period, the participant developed 4-5 personal workarounds for product gaps, suggesting unmet feature needs.

### Key Participant Quotes

> "The activity feed gives me a clear picture of what happened while I was away without reading every message" — Naomi Tanaka, Day 3

> "I've used probably a dozen project management tools and this one has the best learning curve" — Naomi Tanaka, mid-study check-in

> "What I appreciate most is that it doesn't try to do everything, it does its core job well" — Naomi Tanaka, mid-study check-in

> "What frustrates me is that the free tier is so limited you can't really evaluate the product properly" — Naomi Tanaka, Week 2 reflection

### Recommendations

1. Standardize interaction patterns across product sections to reduce the cognitive load of context-switching within the app.
2. Invest in performance optimization — even small delays compounded into significant frustration over a multi-week usage period.
3. Implement intelligent notification grouping and quiet hours to reduce alert fatigue without losing important signals.
