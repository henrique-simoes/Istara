# Field Notes — Search and Navigation Overhaul

**Research Goal:** Reduce support tickets related to 'can't find feature' by 35%
**Product:** Project management platform (TechStart Inc)
**Researcher:** Field Research Team
**Period:** 2026-02-20 to 2026-03-07
**Sessions:** 6

## Session 1

**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Participant:** Kwame Asante (P27)
**Role:** Business Analyst, 2000+ employees
**Setting:** In-person (conference room)
**Duration:** 34 minutes

### Observations

- Switched between two tabs repeatedly to compare information.
- Spent over 2 minutes reading the empty state message before taking action.
- Tried to resize a panel that was fixed-width, suggesting expectations from other tools.
- Participant noted: "I can't customize the columns in the table view, so I'm always scrolling sideways to find what I need"

### Notable Quotes

> "I had to watch a YouTube tutorial to understand the reporting feature, and that shouldn't be necessary" — Kwame Asante

> "The notifications are just noise at this point, I ignore all of them" — Kwame Asante

> "The fact that I can't undo a bulk action makes me nervous every time I use it" — Kwame Asante

> "I tried to set up the integration with Slack three times and it failed silently each time" — Kwame Asante

### Researcher Notes

*The positive reaction to the dashboard suggests it should be the default landing page.*

## Cross-Session Themes

1. **Terminology mismatch** — The vocabulary used by participants differs from product labels, causing friction.
2. **Feature discovery** — Powerful features go unused because participants never encounter them organically.
3. **Collaboration gaps** — Teams resort to external tools (Slack, email) for tasks the product should support.
4. **Mobile avoidance** — Most participants described using mobile only for quick checks, not for substantive work.
5. **Workaround culture** — Participants have developed informal processes outside the product to compensate for gaps.
