**Study Topic:** Dashboard Usability

**Date Range:** 2026-02-04 to 2026-02-17 (14 days)
**Methodology:** Longitudinal self-reported diary study with daily prompts

## Study Protocol

Participant was asked to log their interactions with the product at least once daily,
noting activities performed, emotional reactions, friction points encountered, and
overall mood. Photo/screenshot attachments were encouraged. Weekly reflections were
prompted every 7 days.

## End-of-Study Summary

### Mood Trend

- **Average mood:** 2.5 / 5
- **First half average:** 1.8 / 5
- **Second half average:** 3.4 / 5
- **Days logged:** 11 of 14
- **Overall trend:** Upward

### Emerging Themes

1. **Workaround accumulation** — Over the study period, the participant developed 4-5 personal workarounds for product gaps, suggesting unmet feature needs.
2. **Context-switching tax** — Time lost transitioning between the product and complementary tools (Slack, email, docs) was a recurring theme.
3. **Learning curve trajectory** — Initial confusion gave way to competence, but several interaction patterns remained unintuitive throughout the entire study period.

### Key Participant Quotes

> "I use it every single day, so even small annoyances add up over time" — Michael Torres, Day 7

> "I'm not sure who decided the default view should show everything, it's completely overwhelming" — Michael Torres, Day 7

> "The tag system is incredibly flexible, we use it for priority, department, client, and sprint all at once" — Michael Torres, Day 5

> "The drag-and-drop interface is really satisfying when it works, but sometimes items just snap to the wrong place" — Michael Torres, Week 2 reflection

### Recommendations

1. Create an in-product discovery mechanism (e.g., tips, contextual suggestions) to reduce dependency on social learning.
2. Redesign the mobile experience as a first-class companion app rather than a miniaturized desktop port.
3. Improve search relevance and surface recent items more prominently to support the 'find, don't navigate' behavior observed.
4. Standardize interaction patterns across product sections to reduce the cognitive load of context-switching within the app.
